    DATA DISAGGREGATION

    the new yellow peril

    WHAT IS IT?

    DATA DISAGGREGATION refers to information that breaks down summarized, aggregated data into smaller units or groupings in order to understand the data set better.

    WHO DOES IT AFFECT?

    Although disaggregated data is a general term for data that information that has been compiled from other sources of data, this project will focus on the social impact that data disaggregation has on specifically the Asian American and Pacific Islander communities.

    WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?

    Although Asian American and Pacific Islanders are stereotyped and seen as high-performing in many different fields (like education and health), especially through the lens of the model minority myth, the data used to support that assumption is typically aggregated. It does not reflect discrepancies between each ethnic subgroup, resulting in some struggles and narratives of certain AAPI subgroups to be "unseen" and "unknown".
